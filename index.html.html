The tiles at a glance ›
Three number suits (each 1–9) plus honor tiles. A winning hand is five sets + one pair from 16 tiles (a "set" is three of a kind, or three in a run within one suit). Honors can't form runs.

What "tiles from ready" means ›
Your distance to a finished hand is measured in tiles. Ready (聽牌 / tenpai) means one tile away from winning. "2 from ready" means you still need two useful draws. Good discarding does two things at once: keep that number as low as possible, and keep your hand wide — the more different tiles that can help you, the faster the right one shows up. The coach scores both: which discard keeps you closest, and which leaves the most winning tiles live.

This module trains pure efficiency. It doesn't yet weigh hand value (台) or defense against opponents — those are separate skills we'll build next.

A practice tool: drill it to build instincts you'll use at a real table on your own. It trains tile efficiency (shanten + ukeire) with deterministic math, and self-checks its engine on load. Stats reset when you reload. Built around standard 16-tile Taiwanese structure (5 sets + 1 pair).
